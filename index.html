Top 5 films

1.Kodak Ultramax 400

2. Kodak TMAX 400

3. Kodak Colorplus 200

4. Phoenix 200

5. Kodak Gold 200

Why?

Ultramax - pretty summer vibes, pretty grainy, gives you the film look you may want from analogue photography

TMAX - best BW film ever, perfect grain, very forgiving film

Colorplus - classic, versatile, not too expensive, very pretty colours, not too grainy. Works equally good in point and shoots and manual cameras

Phoenix - my new love. Best for sumemr or sunny days, gives you a pretty red hue from lights sources and red/pink/yellow objects

Kodak Gold - classic, not too expensive, but basically the same as Fuji 200. Not too grainy, works good with pushing/pulling

When?

All of them are best to use during the day, but Ultramax can be used in a poorly lit enviroments on a long exposure setting (B or 1/8)
